admin: full system access
hr: human resources management
qs: quantity surveying operations
billing: invoice and payment management
project_manager: project oversight
finance: financial reporting
reviewer: document review access
approver: approval permissions

From your SQL, the permissions model is this:

admin

gets all permissions on all resources


hr

gets all permissions whose resource is employees


qs

gets permissions for projects, timesheets, bids, deliverables


billing

gets permissions for invoices, expenses


project_manager

gets permissions for projects, timesheets, deliverables


finance

gets permissions for invoices, expenses, projects


reviewer

gets every permission where action is read or update


approver

gets every permission where action is approve


Important limitation:
your current live RLS does not truly enforce this role model for most business tables. The role tables and mappings exist, but the table policies are mostly broad authenticated access. So right now, in practice, many signed-in users can do more than their nominal role suggests unless you tighten RLS further.
